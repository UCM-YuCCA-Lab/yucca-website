Research Focus

The YuCCA Lab focuses on advancing the frontiers of cognitive computing and intelligent automation systems. Our research spans multiple domains including machine learning, human-computer interaction, and autonomous systems. We develop novel computational methods that enable machines to understand, reason, and learn from complex data, with applications in robotics, natural language processing, and decision support systems.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines insights from computer science, cognitive science, and engineering to create intelligent systems that can effectively collaborate with humans in real-world environments.

Active Projects

Project Image

Adaptive Cognitive Architectures for Autonomous Systems

Developing next-generation cognitive architectures that enable autonomous agents to adapt their decision-making strategies based on environmental complexity and task requirements. This project explores bio-inspired learning mechanisms and meta-learning approaches.

Project Image

Human-AI Collaboration in Complex Decision Making

Investigating effective paradigms for human-AI teaming in high-stakes decision-making scenarios. We focus on explainable AI methods, trust calibration, and adaptive interface design to enhance collaborative performance.

Project Image

Multimodal Learning for Robotic Perception

Creating robust perception systems that integrate visual, auditory, and tactile information for improved robotic understanding of unstructured environments. Applications include assistive robotics and manufacturing automation.
