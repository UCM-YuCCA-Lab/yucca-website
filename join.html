Join Our Lab

We are always looking for motivated individuals who are passionate about advancing cognitive computing and automation. Below are opportunities for different types of prospective members.

PhD Students

We are actively recruiting talented PhD students to join our research group. Successful candidates will have the opportunity to work on cutting-edge research problems in cognitive computing, machine learning, and intelligent systems. We seek students with strong technical backgrounds, creative problem-solving abilities, and a genuine passion for research.

What we offer: Full funding through research assistantships, competitive stipends, opportunities to publish in top-tier venues, collaborative research environment, and mentorship for academic career development.

Requirements: Strong programming skills (Python, C++), background in machine learning or related areas, excellent written and verbal communication skills, and previous research experience is a plus.

How to apply: Please send your CV, research statement, transcripts, and contact information for three references to [email to be added]. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Undergraduate and Master's Students

We welcome motivated undergraduate and Master's students from UC Merced and surrounding institutions to join our lab as research assistants. This is an excellent opportunity to gain hands-on research experience, develop technical skills, and explore potential career paths in academia or industry.

What we offer: Mentorship from PhD students and faculty, training in state-of-the-art methods and tools, opportunities to contribute to publications, flexible scheduling to accommodate coursework, and potential for funded positions based on performance.

Requirements: Minimum 10 hours per week commitment, strong academic standing (GPA 3.0+), proficiency in at least one programming language, enthusiasm for learning and collaboration, and ability to work independently.

How to apply: Email your CV, unofficial transcript, and a brief statement (1 paragraph) explaining your interest in our research. We accept applications throughout the year, with positions typically starting at the beginning of each semester.

High School Students

We occasionally host exceptional high school students who demonstrate strong interest and aptitude in computer science and artificial intelligence. These positions provide early exposure to university-level research and can help students determine if research is the right path for them.

What we offer: Introduction to research methodologies, coding projects appropriate for your skill level, mentorship from undergraduate and graduate students, exposure to current AI/ML technologies, and opportunity to build a strong foundation for college applications.

Requirements: Strong interest in computer science and mathematics, basic programming experience (any language), minimum 8-week commitment during summer or academic year, parental consent, and ability to work on-campus or remotely with regular check-ins.

How to apply: Send a brief email introducing yourself, your academic interests, any relevant coursework or projects, and what you hope to learn from the experience. Include your school, grade level, and available time period.

Visiting Scholars and Postdoctoral Researchers

We welcome visiting scholars, postdoctoral researchers, and sabbatical visitors who wish to collaborate on research projects aligned with our lab's interests. Visits can range from short-term collaborations to year-long stays, depending on mutual interests and funding availability.

What we offer: Access to computational resources and lab facilities, collaborative research environment with regular seminars and discussions, opportunities for joint publications and grant proposals, connections with the broader UC Merced research community, and assistance with visa and logistical arrangements when possible.

Requirements: PhD degree in computer science, electrical engineering, or related field, strong publication record in relevant areas, clear research proposal that aligns with lab interests, ability to self-fund or bring external funding (we can assist with identifying funding opportunities), and typically 3-12 month minimum commitment.

How to apply: Please email your CV, research statement outlining proposed collaboration, list of publications, proposed visit duration and timeline, and funding status. Plan to reach out at least 3-6 months before intended visit date.

General Information

For all inquiries, please include "YuCCA Lab - [Position Type]" in your email subject line. We strive to respond to all serious inquiries within 2 weeks. Due to high volume, we may not be able to respond to every email individually, but all applications are carefully reviewed.

We are committed to fostering an inclusive and diverse research environment. We encourage applications from individuals of all backgrounds and particularly welcome those from groups underrepresented in computing.
